HOME ELECTRONIC MEDICAL RECORD (HEMR)
eH4H has taken a US Certified Physician Office Electronic Medical Record (HEMR) and enhanced the EMR to deal with Home Monitoring. The HEMR has the added modules that collect patient information such as: Drug infomation to identify potential interactions and assure medication compliance Nutrition information in the form of industry surveys and scores IADL information to identify Activities of Daily Living deficits Digital "real time" Data Module that collects and records patient data. Also included in our HEMR are module that identify "out lier" data and immediate alerts to Healthcare Professionals and standard protocols that create uniform action steps.
Drug Module This means the collection of drug information to assure future compliance and to check for potential interactions
Nutritional Module
This Module contains nutritional, physical, weight and healthcare indicators collected through a data driven survey. Considering the collected information, action plans will be developed and monitored.
ADL (Activities of Daily Living) Survey Module
The intake survey of IADL scores to establish a baseline to monitor changes in ADLs over time based on an action plan to improve results (What changes
Telehealth Digital Data Monitor (DDM)
The core of the eH4H is the collection of "real time" patient data from medical devices and imported into the HEMR and filtered with edits to identify potential medical problems and alerting healthcare professionals and family
